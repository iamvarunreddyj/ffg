Vocabulary Terms

GENOTYPE
The genes present in the DNA of an organism. We will use a pair of letters (ex: Tt or YY or ss, etc.) to represent genotypes for one particular trait. There are always two letters in the genotype because (as a result of sexual reproduction) one code for the trait comes from mama organism & the other comes from papa organism, so every offspring gets two codes (two letters). Now, turns out there are three possible GENOTYPES - two big letters (like "TT"), one of each ("Tt"), or two lowercase letters ("tt").

HOMOZYGOUSWhen we have two capital or two lowercase letters in the GENOTYPE (ex: TT or tt) it's called HOMOZYGOUS ("homo" means "the same"). Sometimes the term "PURE" is used instead of homozygous.

HETEROZYGOUS When the GENOTYPE is made up of one capital letter & one lowercase letter (ex: Tt) it's called HETEROZYGOUS ("hetero" means "other"). A heterozygous genotype can also be referred to as HYBRID.

PHENOTYPE
How the trait physically shows-up in the organism. Wanna know the simplest way to determine an organism's phenotype ? Look at it.
Examples of phenotypes: blue eyes, brown fur, striped fruit, yellow flowers.

ALLELES
Alternative forms of the same gene. Alleles for a trait are located at corresponding positions on homologous chromosomes. Remember just a second ago when explaining genotypes I said that "one code (letter) comes from ma & one code (letter) comes from pa"? Well "allele" is a fancy word for what I called codes. For example, there is a gene for hair texture (whether hair is curly or straight). One form of the hair texture gene codes for curly hair. A different code for of the same gene makes hair straight. So the gene for hair texture exists as two alleles --- one curly code, and one straight code.

  1. Select the cross type :

    The first step of the experiment would be to select a cross type. Types of cross types available are :

    1. Mono-hybrid cross
    2. Di-hybrid cross
    3. Tri-hybrid cross

    Choose any one cross type from the dropdown list

  2. Select phenotype for the cross.

    Choose phenotype from the dropdown list.


    • If its a Mono-hybrid cross, you will only be asked to choose phenotype once.
    • If its a Di-hybrid cross, you will only be asked to choose two phenotype.
    • If its a Tri-hybrid cross, you will only be asked to choose three phenotype.

  3. Select Dominant and Recessive parent alleles for the cross.

    List of alleles available for the phenotype chosen are displayed under two sections.

    1. Click the respective radio button to choose a dominant allele.
    2. Click the respective radio button to choose a recessive allele.

  4. Depending on the cross type selection you will be asked to chose only one phenotype for parents or multiple.

  5. Generating the outcome.

    1. Once you have chosen parents for our cross, we can then proceed to generate offspring by clicking on the "Generate Offspring" button at the bottom.
    2. You will then be shown your parents selection at the top of the page and the outcome of the experiment right below it.

  6. Using worksheet to predict the outcome of the experiment.

    1. Download the exercise sheet.
    2. Fill the blanks in the sheet with information shown on the outcome page. Sample worksheet can be downloaded here
      • Cross type
      • Phenotype selected
      • Dominant allele
      • Recessive allele
    3. Also write down the notation for each allele.
    4. Now write down the possible combinations of the alleles on the both sides of the punnett square depending on the cross type.
      1. If the cross type is mono-hybrid, then choose 2*2 punnett square.

How to use Punnett squares to predict the outcome.

Here are the basic steps to using a Punnett Square when solving a genetics question.

  1. Determine the genotypes of the parent organisms.

    • For example, if the alleles selected are dominant red eye (+) and recessive white eye (w) then the genetype notation for red eye would be capital A since it is dominant and a for white eye since its recessive.

  2. Write the genotypes of the parents in the form of letters.

    • For example, if the alleles selected are dominant red eye (+) and recessive white eye (w) then, AA x aa.

  3. Draw a punnett square.

  4. "Split" the letters of the genotype for each parent & put them "outside" the punnett square.

  5. Fill and complete the punnett square.
